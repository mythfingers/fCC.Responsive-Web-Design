Timothy Keller

Remembering an irenic voice in an uncertain times.

Brief Timeline of Tim's Life and work

  • 1950: Timothy J. Keller is born in Allentown, Pennsylvania.
  • 1972: Keller graduates from Bucknell University and later attends Gordon-Conwell Theological Seminary, where he earns his M.Div.
  • 1975: Keller marries Kathy Kristy, and together they go on to have three sons.
  • 1984â€“1989: Keller serves as a professor of practical theology at Westminster Theological Seminary in Philadelphia, while also pastoring a small church in Hopewell, Virginia.
  • 1989: Keller is called to start a new church in New York City. He co-founds Redeemer Presbyterian Church in Manhattan with a focus on urban ministry and reaching skeptics. Redeemer grows rapidly, becoming one of the most influential churches in the city.
  • 2001: Keller publishes The Reason for God, addressing common doubts and presenting a rational case for Christian belief. The book becomes a bestseller and is widely acclaimed for its thoughtful engagement with skeptics.
  • 2003: Keller co-founds the Gospel Coalition, a network of Reformed evangelical churches focused on promoting theological faithfulness, mission, and cultural engagement.
  • 2008: Keller publishes The Prodigal God, which explores the parable of the Prodigal Son and its message about grace and the gospel.
  • 2017: Keller steps down as Senior Pastor of Redeemer to focus on teaching, writing, and mentoring younger pastors, though he continues to preach.
  • 2020: Keller announces he has been diagnosed with pancreatic cancer but continues to write, speak, and teach.
  • May 19, 2023: Tim Keller passes away, leaving behind a legacy of influential urban ministry, church planting, and Christian apologetics.
